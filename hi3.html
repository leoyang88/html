The Southern Colonies

Colonies in the Southern region:

  • Maryland, MD. Was founded by George Calvert aka Lord Baltimore for religious freedom for Catholics.
  • North Carolina, NC. Was founded by a group of proprietors for trade and profits.
  • South Carolina, SC. Also founded by a group of proprietors for trade and profits.
  • Georgia, GA. Founded by James Oglethorpe as a debtor colony and a meat shield for South Carolina from the Native Americans.
  • Virginia, VA. Was founded by John Smith for trade and profits.

Political:

  • People usually invested in joint-stock companies to start a colony. The Southern Colonies are no different.
  • The British Crown appoints the governor of the Royal Colonies.
  • The first idea of a representative government was the Virginia House of Burgesses.

Economic:

  • The Southerners grew tobacco as a cash crop.
  • Slavery in the Southern Colonies was growing as there were a lot of big farms that required the labor of many humans.
  • The Southern Colonies had the agrarian cash crop system which grew tobacco, cotton, and indigo.

Geographic:

  • The Southern Colonies had flat land and was hot which made it perfect for growing crops such as tobacco.
  • There were a lot of plantations for cash crops. The plantations relied on slavery.
  • Down in the South, the growing seasons were long. This is another thing that attributes to the success of the cash crops in the South.

Society:

  • Maryland was originally founded for religious freedom for Catholics in Anglican England.
  • Maryland then passed the Maryland Act of Toleration which protected all Christians.
  • There were clear class divisions, the distances impeded growth of schools, and few cities developed in the South.
