The New England Colonies

Colonies in the New England:

  • New Hampshire, NH. Founded by John Mason for those who wanted to escape the relgious and economic rules in Massachusetts.
  • Massachusetts, MA. Founded by John Winthrop for religious freedom for Puritans.
  • Connecticut, CT. Founded by Thomas Hooker for religious and economic Freedom.
  • Rhode Island, RI. Founded by Roger Williams for religious freedom.

Political:

  • All land-owning white Christian males may vote.
  • Government authority came from the King.
  • There were torn meeting houses for religious worship and conducting town business.

Economic:

  • Long winters, short summers, and rocky soil made farming difficult. Therefore, they didn't use farming to make money.
  • The humans living in New England then decided to trade with their mother country, England. They exported fish, furs, and naval stores. In return, they received glass, linens, hardware, machinery, naval instruments, and other household items.
  • Later on, England placed taxes on fish to protect their fisheries and meat to protect their agriculture.

Geographic:

  • New England had a lot of forests and mountains. The soil was rocky, and they were near the Atlantic Ocean. New England also had lots of coal.
  • Humans relied on the lumber fromm the forests. They also fished and whaled as the area they were in had short growing seasons. New England was urban.
  • Maine was not a colony at the time as it was mainly the place for the overflow from MA.

Society

  • The first settlers in the New England region were the Pilgrims. They disagreed with the religious teaching of the Anglican Church and decided the leave.
  • The New England area had guaranteed public education and the life expectancy was longer.
  • If you were in the Puritan society, church attendance was mandatory.
